There are powers which [...] man, not willing that he should be saved, that they may [...]. For if man is saved, there will be no sacrifices [...] and animals will not be offered to the powers. Indeed, those to whom sacrifices were offered were animals, for they were offered up alive. When they were offered up, they died. As for man, he was offered up to God dead, and he lived.
