Thus David the Prophet said, “A people whom I do not know shall serve Me; at the mere hearing of the ear they shall answer Me.” And to the Jews he said, “The children of the stranger have been false to me, the children of the stranger have become old and have travelled haltingly on their path. God liveth, and blessed [is] my God.” When he saith unto thee, “God liveth,” he speaketh of His Godhead, and when he saith unto thee “and blessed [is] my God,” he speaketh concerning His putting on the flesh. And again he speaketh concerning His putting on the flesh in Isaiah the Prophet, saying, “Who is this glorious One who cometh forth from Edom, Adônâi, Who came down from heaven, and put on the things of Basôr, glorious in majesty?” When he saith “glorious” he refereth to His sweet odour; and when he saith “Adônâi,” he meaneth the Word of the Father Who was before the world, the Son of God; when he saith “He put on the things of Basôr, the glorious in majesty,” he indicateth clearly the body of Adam.
