At  the  time  of  cock-crow,  let  him  pray  first  over  the    s 
water  ;  and  let  the  water  be  either  running  through  the 
tank  or  running  (down)  upon  it      And  let  it  be  thus 
unless  there  was  urgent  necessity.     If  there  was  urgent 
necessity,  he  shall  pour  in  water  which  is  found.     Let 
them   undress,  and   they  shall   begin   by   baptising  the  lo 
little  children  first.     And  he  who  can  speak  for  himself 
and  take  oath  shall  speak,  and  for  them  who  cannot  let 
their  parents  speak,  or  one  of  the  family.     Afterwards 
they  shall  baptise  the  grown  men,  and  the  women  last : 
and  they  shall  unloose  their  hair  and   lay  aside   their  ^5 
ornaments  of  gold  which  they  wear,  and  let  no  one  go 
down  to  the  water  having  any  alien  thing.
